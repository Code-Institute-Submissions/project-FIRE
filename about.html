PE Ratio

Price to earnings, or the P/E ratio, is the time tested benchmark of a well functioning company. It is calculated by taking the market cap of a company divided by its full year earnings. A low PE means that the company is undervalued while a high P/E often means that a company is over valued. However, this is only a gauge as there are many other factors to a good company.

Examples of low P/E stocks

  • ORCL - Oracle Corporation (16x)
  • HD - Home Depot (22x)
  • DAL - Delta Air Lines (4x)
  • GM - General Motors Company (8x)
  • AAPL - Apple Inc (24x)
  • BAC - Bank of America Corp (11x)

Dividends

Cash is king. So it is even sweeter when your investment pays you a quarterly paycheck! Did you know that if you invest an initial sum of $10,000 and with an additional $10,000 topup every year, 4% P.A. compounded would be $615,000 in 30 years!

Examples of Dividend stocks

  • WFC - Wells Fargo & Company (6.74% P.A.)
  • IBM - International Business Machines (5.43% P.A.)
  • REG - Regency Centers (5.82% P.A.)
  • T - AT&T Inc (6.88% P.A.)
  • USB - US Bancorp (4.71% P.A.)
