Disruptors

A growth company cannot be valued the same way you look at established companies. A growth company will often sacrifice earnings and voluntarily incur losses to grow their market share. Growth companies are like toddlers, they need all the financial support to grow into profitable companies.

For example, Amazon Inc, now the largest e-commerce platform in the world, used to make little to no money for 20 years all in the name of growth and market domination.

Examples of Disruptors

  • TSLA - Tesla Inc.
  • SQ - Square Inc.
  • LMND - Lemonade Inc.
  • CRSP - Crispr Therapeutics AG

Notable Mentions (Unlisted)

  • Byte Dance - TikTok
  • Ant Financial - AliPay
  • SpaceX and Starlink

Sales Growth

To fully dominate a market, a company focused on growth has to grow their earnings even if they do not turn a profit. For example, if a company with an initial revenue of $1b grows at a rate of 30% P.A, their revenue will balloon to over $51b in just 15 years!

Examples of high growth stocks

  • NFLX - Netflix Inc.(27%)
  • TSLA - Tesla Inc. (31%)
  • AMD - Advanced Micro Devices Inc. (40%)
  • SQ - Square Inc. (44%)
  • ZM -Zoom Video Communications Inc. (169%)
